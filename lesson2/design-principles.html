Design Principles Document

Josh R. Hamilton

Emphasis and Contrast

3M

3m.com

The color palette used by 3M here draws our attention to a few spots. The first is the logo, which is pure red against white. The second is the text, which is white against a deep blue. The third, and most important, is the "call to action", which is the same color as the logo. These combine to create emphasis on the most important parts of the page.

Visual Balance

Disneyland

disneyland.disney.go.com

The Hero Image, which is a vibrant view of some of Disneyland's attractions, is heavily weighted against the text below it. Above the image is the Disneyland logo, which we are all familiar with. This creates a heirarchy of viewing, where we first realize which company this is, then notice how amazing the park looks, and last we want to read more.

Rule of Thirds

Coca Cola

us.coca-cola.com

Coca Cola's mobile view of this image places the individual's face right in the upper-right vertex. Not only does this appeal to the golden ratio, it provides enough room for the introductory text to nestle comfortably. After appreciating this design decision, the user is greeted by some welcoming text, inviting the reader to explore the product.
